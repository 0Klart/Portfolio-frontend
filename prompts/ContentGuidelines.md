# Content Guidelines

## Source policy

- Use only public, non-sensitive information from user-provided sources or repository-visible content.
- Do not guess dates, employers, education, titles, achievements, or technologies.
- If a source is incomplete or blocked, say less rather than inventing details.

## Tone

- Write in a professional, direct, modern tone.
- Prefer concrete statements over generic self-promotion.
- Keep portfolio copy focused on work, strengths, stack, and impact.

## Privacy boundaries

- Do not surface private CV content by default.
- Avoid personal addresses, phone numbers, or sensitive identifiers.
- Use public profile links and project evidence instead of downloadable personal documents when possible.

## Priorities

1. Credibility
2. Clarity
3. Relevance to employers and collaborators
4. Visual consistency
